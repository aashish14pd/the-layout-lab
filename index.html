What is Physical Design?

Physical Design is the process of transforming a synthesised netlist into a manufacturable silicon layout. It bridges the gap between logical abstraction and silicon reality — ensuring the chip meets every timing, power, and area requirement.

In modern SoC design, PD is one of the most critical phases of the ASIC cycle. Engineers use tools like Synopsys Fusion Compiler, ICC2, and Cadence Innovus to implement designs from floorplanning through GDSII tape-out.

The flow must handle millions of standard cells while satisfying strict constraints on setup/hold timing, IR drop, congestion, and design rule compliance set by the target foundry process node.

Performance

Ensures critical paths meet frequency targets through timing-driven placement and routing.

Power Integrity

Power planning prevents IR drop and electromigration across the full supply network.

Area Optimisation

Efficient floorplanning and placement reduce die area and lower per-unit cost.

DRC / LVS Sign-off

Physical verification ensures the layout obeys fab rules and matches the schematic.

01 — DRC

Design Rule Check

DRC verifies that every shape in the layout complies with the foundry's manufacturing rules — minimum width, spacing, enclosure, and density rules for every metal layer, via, and device. Violations must be resolved before tape-out.

Min WidthMin SpacingEnclosureMetal DensityVia Rules

02 — LVS

Layout vs Schematic

LVS compares the transistor-level netlist extracted from the layout against the original schematic netlist. It confirms that the physical layout is electrically equivalent to the intended design, catching missing connections, shorts, and device mismatches.

Netlist ExtractionConnectivityDevice MatchingShort / Open

03 — ERC

Electrical Rule Check

ERC identifies electrical violations that DRC misses — antenna effects, floating gates, undriven inputs, electrostatic discharge (ESD) risks, and latch-up susceptibility. These checks ensure the chip will not be damaged during fabrication or in the field.

AntennaESDLatch-upFloating Nets

04 — PEX

Parasitic Extraction

PEX extracts the resistance and capacitance of every routed net from the final layout geometry and outputs a SPEF file. These parasitics feed into STA and signal integrity analysis to give accurate, post-layout timing and power numbers.

05 — ANTENNA CHECK

Antenna Rule Check

During plasma etching, long metal wires accumulate charge that can destroy thin gate oxides. Antenna checks verify the antenna ratio (metal area / gate area) stays within foundry limits. Fixes include metal jogs, antenna diodes, or re-routing affected nets.

06 — SIGN-OFF

Physical Sign-off

Final sign-off integrates clean DRC, LVS, ERC, PEX, and STA results across all PVT corners. The GDSII or OASIS file is submitted to the foundry only after every check passes. A single DRC violation at this stage can delay a tape-out by weeks.
